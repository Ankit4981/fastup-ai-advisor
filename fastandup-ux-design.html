Section 01

CONVERSATION FLOW

A 6-step guided journey from greeting to personalised recommendation. Each step is designed to feel like a conversation with a knowledgeable friend — not a form.

0
Trigger · Bot Initiates
👋 Greeting & Intent Setting
The bot opens with warmth and a clear promise — answer 4 questions, get the perfect product. Sets expectations and reduces drop-off anxiety.
Hey! I'm Fast&Up's AI Advisor. Answer 4 quick questions and I'll find the perfect supplement for you — no guesswork, just results. Ready? 🚀
Yes, let's go! What products do you have? How does this work?
1
Question 1 of 4 · Primary Filter
🎯 What's your main goal?
The most important dimension. This determines the entire product category and anchors all subsequent logic. If a user can't find their goal in the options, the fallback is "General wellness."
Build muscle 💪 Improve endurance 🏃 Boost energy & immunity ⚡ Lose weight 🔥 General wellness 🌿
Design note: Options are worded from the user's perspective (outcome-first), not the product's perspective — avoids supplement jargon entirely at this stage.
2
Question 2 of 4 · Activity Filter
🏋️ How active are you?
Determines formulation intensity — a runner needs electrolytes while a sedentary professional needs daily vitamins. Also cross-references with goal to refine stack logic.
Gym 4–5× a week 🏋️ Running or cycling 🚴 Yoga & light exercise 🧘 Not very active 🪑
Design note: Emojis serve as rapid visual anchors — users often scan before reading. Each emoji immediately communicates the activity category.
3
Question 3 of 4 · Diet Filter
🌱 What's your diet type?
Critical for India's dietary diversity. This filter eliminates entire product sub-ranges and prevents the most damaging type of wrong recommendation — products incompatible with a user's ethics or physiology.
Vegetarian 🌱 Vegan 🌿 Non-Vegetarian 🍗 Jain 🙏
Design note: Jain is included as a distinct filter — a deliberate differentiator for the Indian market that no competitor currently offers in their recommendation flow.
4
Question 4 of 4 · Experience Filter
🧪 Are you new to supplements?
Controls stack complexity and tone. Beginners get a single, easy entry-point product with simple instructions. Experienced users can handle a full pre/during/post stack with detailed rationale.
Yes, I'm new to this I've used supplements before
Design note: Deliberately kept binary to reduce friction. The distinction between "beginner" and "advanced" is handled internally by the decision engine — the user doesn't need to self-classify their expertise level.
5
AI Processing · Engine Output
🧠 Personalised Recommendation Card
Claude AI matches the 4 inputs against the product logic matrix, generates a personalised explanation, and presents a product card with name, reason-why, relevant tags, and a clear CTA.
Based on what you've told me — [goal + activity + diet] — I've found exactly what you need. Here's why this product is perfect for your specific situation...
Design note: The recommendation always explains WHY before stating WHAT. This builds trust and reduces the "is this just a sales pitch?" doubt in the user's mind.
6
Feedback Loop · Session Close
👍 Satisfaction & Follow-up
Was this helpful? Binary feedback drives model improvement. If 👎, the bot offers 2 alternatives. If 👍, it provides a buy link and an optional "tell me more" prompt to deepen engagement.
👍 This is perfect! Show me alternatives How do I take it? 💊 Start over 🔄

Beginner Path

Beginners always receive a single product recommendation. The engine avoids stack complexity for new users. The recommendation card uses plain language and explains basic usage instructions.

Example: New gym-goer, veg → ProTotal Plant only, not the full pre/post stack
Experienced Path

Experienced users receive a full stack recommendation with timing instructions (pre/during/post). The card uses more technical language and explains synergies between products.

Example: Experienced runner, non-veg → Reload + Energy Gel + Recover stack
Edge Case Handling
Ambiguous Goal

If the user selects "I'm not sure," the bot asks one clarifying question: "Is your main priority how you look, how you perform, or how you feel?" — narrows to a category without a full restart.

Conflicting Inputs

If goal=weight loss but activity=gym 5×, the engine weights goal first and recommends a lean-muscle stack (protein + Charge+) rather than a generic weight loss product.

Low Confidence

When confidence score is medium (●●○), the bot surfaces 2 options and explains the trade-off: "If you prioritise X, go with Product A. If Y matters more, Product B fits better."

Section 05

UX DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Six core principles that govern every design decision in this chatbot — from the order of questions to the wording of recommendations.

01
🗣️
Conversational, Not Clinical
Every question is worded from the user's lived experience — "feel tired all the time" not "energy deficiency." No supplement jargon until the recommendation. The chatbot sounds like a friend who happens to know nutrition, not a bot running a script.
02
🎯
Progressive Disclosure
One question at a time, never a form. Each answer is acknowledged before the next question appears. This reduces cognitive load and keeps completion rates high — users don't feel interrogated, they feel heard.
03
💡
Why Before What
The recommendation always explains WHY a product fits this specific person before naming the product. This builds trust, prevents "is this just advertising?" scepticism, and helps users internalise the logic — making them better at choosing on their own next time.
04
🛡️
Safety-First for Beginners
First-time users always get a single product, plain language, and a simple usage instruction. The chatbot never overwhelms a beginner with a complex stack. Trust is built with simplicity first — advanced stacks are introduced only after the user reports success.
05
🇮🇳
India-First Design Decisions
Vegetarian/Vegan/Jain diet filters, Hindi language support in Phase 2, INR pricing, awareness that "not very active" is a valid and common lifestyle state — every decision acknowledges India's unique dietary culture and fitness landscape.
06
🔁
Feedback-Driven Improvement
Every recommendation ends with a 👍/👎 signal. Negative feedback triggers an alternatives flow and flags the session for review. Over time, this creates a self-improving system — the chatbot gets smarter with every conversation, without manual retraining.
Design Philosophy
"The best interface is the one that makes a complex decision feel effortless. This chatbot doesn't replace the user's judgment — it amplifies it."
4 questions → 1 perfect recommendation
